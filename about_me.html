book cover author summary & my thoughts
Parable of the Sower Octavia E. Butler Parable of the Sower is one of the best books I've read recently, and what stood out to me the most was the philosophy. The book is a dystopia, so things tend to fall apart in pretty awful ways for the protagonist Lauren, but in the process of experiencing these events she builds her own concept of religion and the world which she compiles into the book of Earthseed. Her concept of Earthseed is, in my eyes, a reflection of Octavia's views of the world herself, and the concept of change being represented as a sort of divine force which has no inherent morality taught me to be far more flexible in my own life. In the words of Lauren, "The only lasting truth is Change. God is Change."
Oryx and Crake Margaret Atwood I read this book when I was pretty young, still in highschool. It was the first dystopia that rang with me, and it was the depth of the characters and the grittiness of the environment that made me fall in love with the genre. Margaret Atwood is in my eyes a master of dystopian fiction, and Oryx and Crake was the first work of hers that drew me into her collection of books and short stories. I wrote a flash fiction piece called "House" inspired by the style in her prose poem "Bread."
The Alchemist Pablo Coelho Another book that I read when I was young, The Alchemist presents a beautiful picture of the world in a way that unifies people and cultures in contrast to most books. The story is about a shepherd named Santiago who goes on a journey to find his personal legend, and by the end of it discovers something far bigger-- the soul of the world.
Jane Eyre Charlotte Bronte Jane Eyre is actually not one of my favorite books, but I thought I might include it just because I think it had a silly plot. The ending is so unserious. I won't spoil anything but basically it's about a governess falling in love with her employer. Even the most mundane thing like waking up in the middle of the night will literally be described in painstaking detail, for pages. It's a great book for literary analysis!
